# ACEF 5-YEAR PROGRAMME OF WORK: 2025-2030

This 5-year programme of work builds upon the 1-year strategic plan, scaling up activities and impact. It is structured around the organization's core areas of interest, integrating youth empowerment and policy advocacy throughout.

**Overarching Goal**: To establish ACEF as a leading youth-led organization in Africa, driving impactful climate and environmental solutions and empowering a generation of young leaders and green entrepreneurs across the continent.

## Programme Pillars

### Pillar 1: Climate Resilience & Adaptation
- **Objective 1.1 (2025-2026)**: Implement pilot climate adaptation projects (e.g., rainwater harvesting, drought-resistant crops, early warning systems) in 3 vulnerable communities, directly involving youth in planning and implementation.
- **Objective 1.2 (2027-2028)**: Scale up successful pilot projects to 10 additional communities, demonstrating replicable models for youth-led adaptation.
- **Objective 1.3 (2029-2030)**: Develop and advocate for national and regional adaptation plans that explicitly integrate youth-led solutions and indigenous knowledge.
- **Activities**: Community-based adaptation training, climate-smart agriculture demonstrations, resilient WASH infrastructure development, ecosystem-based adaptation initiatives.

### Pillar 2: Climate Mitigation & Sustainable Energy
- **Objective 2.1 (2025-2027)**: Launch "Green Energy Youth Hubs" in 3 African cities, providing training on renewable energy technologies (solar, small-scale wind) and business development for youth.
- **Objective 2.2 (2028-2030)**: Facilitate the establishment of 20 youth-led renewable energy micro-enterprises, contributing to increased energy access in underserved areas.
- **Activities**: Renewable energy workshops, promoting energy efficiency, supporting reforestation/afforestation initiatives, advocating for youth-inclusive green finance mechanisms.

### Pillar 3: Climate Migration, Peace & Security
- **Objective 3.1 (2026-2027)**: Conduct research and publish a report on the nexus of climate change, mobility, and conflict in 3 key African sub-regions, highlighting youth-specific vulnerabilities and opportunities.
- **Objective 3.2 (2027-2029)**: Organize regional youth dialogues on climate security, fostering cross-border collaboration and peacebuilding initiatives.
- **Objective 3.3 (2029-2030)**: Advocate for the inclusion of youth perspectives and climate-induced mobility considerations in national and regional peace and security frameworks.
- **Activities**: Awareness campaigns on climate migration, supporting peacebuilding initiatives through environmental cooperation, advocating for safe and dignified climate mobility.

### Pillar 4: Chemicals & Waste Management
- **Objective 4.1 (2025-2026)**: Implement "Youth for Zero Waste" campaigns in 5 urban and peri-urban areas, promoting waste segregation, recycling, and upcycling among youth.
- **Objective 4.2 (2027-2028)**: Establish 3 youth-led waste collection and recycling cooperatives, creating green jobs and reducing pollution.
- **Objective 4.3 (2028-2030)**: Engage with national governments and industries to advocate for stronger policies on chemical management and circular economy principles, leveraging ACEF's SAICM and BRS and INC accreditations.
- **Activities**: Waste-to-value workshops, advocating for responsible chemical use, promoting sustainable consumption and production patterns.

### Pillar 5: Natural Resources Conservation (Terrestrial & Aquatic)
- **Objective 5.1 (2025-2027)**: Launch "Youth Guardians of Nature" programs in 5 critical biodiversity hotspots, empowering youth in monitoring, conservation, and sustainable resource management.
- **Objective 5.2 (2027-2029)**: Support the establishment of 10 youth-led community nature-based enterprises (e.g., ecotourism, sustainable forestry, artisanal fishing).
- **Objective 5.3 (2029-2030)**: Advocate for youth representation in national and regional natural resource management bodies and the integration of traditional ecological knowledge.
- **Activities**: Tree planting drives, river/coastal clean-ups, promoting sustainable land and water management practices, advocating for protected areas.

### Pillar 6: WASH (Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene)
- **Objective 6.1 (2025-2027)**: Conduct WASH needs assessments in 5 water-stressed communities, with a focus on climate change impacts and youth-specific vulnerabilities.
- **Objective 6.2 (2027-2029)**: Implement 5 youth-led sustainable WASH projects (e.g., solar-powered boreholes, ecological sanitation, hygiene education campaigns).
- **Objective 6.3 (2029-2030)**: Advocate for climate-resilient WASH policies and infrastructure, ensuring equitable access for all, particularly in rural and marginalized areas.
- **Activities**: Water conservation awareness, promoting safe sanitation practices, advocating for integrated water resource management.

### Pillar 7: Agriculture & Food Security
- **Objective 7.1 (2025-2027)**: Establish "Youth Agri-Climate Innovation Labs" in 3 agricultural regions, training youth in climate-smart agriculture techniques and sustainable food systems.
- **Objective 7.2 (2027-2029)**: Support 15 youth-led agricultural cooperatives and agri-businesses, promoting resilient crop varieties, agroforestry, and local food value chains.
- **Objective 7.3 (2029-2030)**: Advocate for youth-inclusive policies that strengthen food security, promote sustainable land use, and reduce post-harvest losses.
- **Activities**: Training on organic farming, permaculture, food processing, market access for youth farmers.

### Pillar 8: Education Access
- **Objective 8.1 (2025-2026)**: Develop and pilot a comprehensive "Climate & Environment Literacy" curriculum for schools and non-formal education settings, tailored to African contexts.
- **Objective 8.2 (2027-2028)**: Train 100 educators (teachers, community facilitators) to deliver climate and environmental education, reaching over 5,000 youth.
- **Objective 8.3 (2028-2030)**: Advocate for the integration of climate and environmental education into national curricula across key African countries.
- **Activities**: Developing educational materials, online learning platforms, environmental clubs in schools, public awareness campaigns.

### Pillar 9: Health Access
- **Objective 10.1 (2025-2027)**: Conduct community health needs assessments in 5 communities, specifically mapping environmental health risks (e.g., air pollution, waterborne diseases, vector-borne diseases due to climate change) and access to health services.
- **Objective 10.2 (2027-2029)**: Implement 5 youth-led environmental health promotion projects, focusing on community awareness campaigns, clean cooking solutions, and improved sanitation to reduce environment-related illnesses.
- **Objective 10.3 (2029-2030)**: Advocate for national and regional policies that integrate climate change considerations into health strategies, including resilient health infrastructure and early warning systems for climate-sensitive diseases.
- **Activities**: Training youth as community health advocates, promoting sustainable and healthy living practices, advocating for climate-resilient primary healthcare, facilitating access to environmental health information.

### Pillar 10: Youth Empowerment & Job Creation (Cross-Cutting)
- **Objective 9.1 (2025-2030)**: Integrate green skills training and entrepreneurship development into all relevant program pillars.
- **Objective 9.2 (2026-2028)**: Establish a "Green Jobs Incubator" for African youth, providing mentorship, seed funding, and market linkages for climate and environment-focused businesses.
- **Objective 9.3 (2029-2030)**: Create a robust alumni network of empowered youth leaders and entrepreneurs to sustain impact and foster peer learning.
- **Activities**: Mentorship programs, business development support, facilitating access to green finance, policy advocacy for youth-friendly green economy frameworks.

## Cross-Cutting Strategies
- **Research & Policy Advocacy**: Continuously leverage ACEF's UN accreditations to submit impactful position papers, organize side events, and ensure strong youth representation in global and regional policy discussions related to all program pillars.
- **Partnerships & Collaboration**: Actively seek and cultivate strategic partnerships with UN agencies, governmental bodies, civil society organizations, academic institutions, and the private sector to amplify reach and impact.
- **Research & Knowledge Sharing**: Conduct and disseminate research on youth-led climate and environmental solutions, best practices, and policy recommendations. Develop and maintain a comprehensive online knowledge hub.
- **Resource Mobilization**: Implement a diversified and robust fundraising strategy, including grant applications, individual giving, corporate partnerships, and innovative financing mechanisms, to ensure long-term sustainability.
- **Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL)**: Establish a robust MEL framework to track progress, measure impact, and foster continuous learning and adaptation across all programs. Regular reporting to the Board and donors.
- **Communications & Visibility**: Develop and implement a proactive communications strategy to raise ACEF's profile, showcase youth impact, and inspire broader engagement through digital platforms, media, and events.
